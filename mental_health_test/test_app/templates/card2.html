Common Mental Health Disorders

Mental health disorders are varied and affect people differently. Some of the most common include:

1. Anxiety Disorders
Characterized by excessive fear, worry, or panic. This category includes generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), social anxiety, and panic disorders.

2. Depression
Symptoms include persistent sadness, loss of interest in activities, fatigue, and feelings of worthlessness. If untreated, depression can lead to severe consequences, such as suicide.

3. Bipolar Disorder
Involves extreme mood swings ranging from depressive lows to manic highs, affecting energy levels, decision-making, and relationships.

4. Schizophrenia and Psychotic Disorders
Involves distorted thinking, perceptions, and behaviors, such as hallucinations and delusions.

5. Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
Develops after experiencing or witnessing traumatic events. Symptoms include flashbacks, nightmares, and severe anxiety.

6. Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
Characterized by intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions).

Understanding these common disorders, promoting awareness, and reducing stigma can encourage individuals to seek help and receive the support they need for effective management and improved quality of life.
